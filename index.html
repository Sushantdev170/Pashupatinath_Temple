Pashupatinath Temple

Pashupatinath is a significant Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, attracting hundreds of elderly followers annually who seek shelter for the final weeks of their lives.

The main temple is a masterful architectural work featuring a golden spire and silver-covered doors. Only Hindus may enter the main temple.

The eastern bank hosts religious buildings devoted to Shiva, while the right bank of the Bagmati has platforms for open-air cremations.

Monkeys and deer roam the complex, and Sadhus—wandering ascetic yogis—are a common sight.
